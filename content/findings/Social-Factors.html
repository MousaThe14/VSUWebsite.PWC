Food Access

According to EcoDistricts (2016), a food desert is a community where more than 20 percent of residents are living below the poverty threshold and at least 33 percent of the population lives more than a mile from a supermarket.

Pollution

Pollution is found to have multiple ties to major health issues. It can serve as an indicator for later in life chronic issues such as heart and respiratory diseases.
Air pollution has been shown to have negative impacts on respiratory and cardiovascular health regardless of short term or chronic exposure.
Long term exposure has been shown to have links to diabetes. Specifically, traffic-related air pollution has been shown as a risk factor for Type 2 Diabetes.
